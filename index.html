Legal record – summary

The legal timeline derived from the base logs begins with the immediate post–May 9 crackdown. In May 2023, reporting records arrest in Islamabad, a release order from the Islamabad High Court on 25 May, same-day re-arrest outside Adiala Jail, and judicial remand by an anti-terrorism court on 30 May. June through September 2023 then document a shift from investigation context and police remand toward continuing judicial proceedings and denial of further physical remand.

Through late 2023 and 2024, the legal picture becomes one of sustained process rather than resolution. November 2023 reporting records indictment. January 2024 adds new May 9-related investigative exposure, while February 2024 shows proceedings being held inside Kot Lakhpat Jail. March 2024 introduces the parliamentary-rights dimension: the Senate issued production orders for his attendance while detained, and later reporting and party posts repeatedly alleged that such orders were ignored.

January to March 2025 is the densest procedural cluster before the conviction wave. The record captures renewed production orders, non-compliance complaints, Senate committee scrutiny, a court hearing concerning production orders, and public statements by family and party accounts that detention and treatment were both becoming more serious. On 2 May 2025, the Supreme Court granted bail after long detention; subsequent reporting on 6 May said that conspiracy charges in one May 9 case were still not proven.

The heaviest conviction phase in the source basis falls between July and December 2025. Reporting records a 10-year sentence in July 2025, disqualification from the Senate later that month, appeal proceedings in August, a further 10-year sentence in September, bail in three cases in October, and additional 10-year sentences in December 2025. By January to April 2026, the dominant legal theme is no longer new conviction reporting but delayed appeals and continued incarceration.

Health record – summary

The health timeline derived from the base logs shows a slower, cumulative pattern. The first strong month is March 2024, when the Al Jazeera family-impact feature documented detention-related health concerns. For much of 2023 and early 2024, there is little discrete health reporting in the first-pass logs, which is itself important: it suggests that the record becomes more visible only when conditions worsen or when public appeals intensify.

By the end of 2024 and beginning of 2025, public posts begin to record more concrete medical concerns. A 31 December 2024 PTI post says he had been hospitalised because of a heart issue after 605 days in jail. A 23 January 2025 PTI post then says family members reported heart disease. On 21 March 2025, Geo News reported that he had three stents in his heart while Senate committee proceedings were criticising his non-production.

August 2025 is the first major hospitalisation cluster in the news record. Dawn reported worsening kidney infection, gallstones, major weight loss, and later transfer to PKLI. The X/Twitter evidence log shows this health reporting being amplified the same week by news accounts and supporter/family-linked accounts. Although legal reporting dominates the remainder of 2025, the health risk never fully disappears from the record.

April 2026 is the most concentrated and urgent health-alert period in the combined source set. The health timeline states that multiple outlets and public posts described stage-three kidney disease, continuing deterioration, claims that treatment was not possible in jail, return from hospital to jail, and calls for urgent transfer or medical bail. Because this April 2026 cluster mixes media reporting with party and family posts, each entry should be cross-checked against hospital records, court orders, lawyer statements, and any available independent medical documents before final external filing.

Independent international reference

IPU case PAK-29

The October 2025 Inter-Parliamentary Union decision lists Ejaz Chaudhary within a collective Pakistan case concerning five PTI parliamentarians.

What the IPU highlights

The decision records concern about detention conditions, healthcare, due process, repeated FIRs, and the need for direct parliamentary or trial-observation engagement.

Why it matters

It serves as an independent international reference that can be used alongside press reports, family statements, and medical or court records.
